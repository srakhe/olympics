Description

The Korean sport of taekwondo first appeared on the Olympic stage in Korea, as a demonstration sport at the 1988 Seoul Olympics. It reappeared as a demonstration in 1992, before being granted official status for the 2000 Games. In order to limit the number of total Olympic competitors, just four weight categories have been contested per gender, while eight are held at World Championships.

At the Olympics the sports has been dominated by Korea, with 19 medals and 12 gold medals, and China, with 10 medals and 7 gold medals. Three taekwondoka have earned two golds and a bronze: Hadi Saei (Iran), Steven Lopez (US), and Hwang Gyeong-Seon (South Korea), with María Espinoza also winning three medals, with one of each color.

Taekwondo is governed worldwide by the World Taekwondo (WT), previously known as World Taekwondo Federation (WTF), which was formed on 28 May 1973 in Seoul. On 17 July 1980 the International Olympic Committee (IOC) recognized the federation at the 83rd IOC Session in Moskva. As of 2020 World Taekwondo has 210 member nations affiliated.
